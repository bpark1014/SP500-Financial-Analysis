# S&P 500 Financial Analysis

## Overview
I built this project to showcase my data analysis skills and apply what I learned about financial markets to a real dataset, as well as demonstrate a portion of my technical capabilities. I focused on analyzing market sector trends in profitability, revenue growth, and market capitalization and made observations based on my findings below.

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel - Pivot tables, charts, xlookup, dashboard
- SQL - SQLite for querying the dataset

## What Were My Main Questions
- Which sectors are the most profitable?
- Which sectors are growing the fastest?
- Which companies dominate the market/certain sectors?
- What is the distribution of S&P500 companies across the various sectors?

## Findings
Communication Services surprised me the most. Despite having one of the fewest number of companies of any sector at just 22, it generated the highest average EBITDA at $25.5 billion. This means that the gap is almost entirely driven by a handful of massive players like Alphabet and Meta.

Financial Services and Technology are the fastest growing sectors at a revenue growth of 12.3% and 11.6% respectively, while Energy and Consumer Defensive are growing roughly 1%. This speaks a lot to how mature those industries are and how society is moving towards a more modern, tech-focused economy 

Further enforcing the previous point, the top 4 companies are all tech firms whose sum value is more than $15 trillion dollars. This further reflects the current economy's desire for innovation and modernization.

## Data Source
S&P 500 Stocks Dataset from Kaggle
